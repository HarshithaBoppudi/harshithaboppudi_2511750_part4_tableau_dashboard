# Dashboard Story — Executive Sales Performance Review

**Audience:** Leadership team
**Period covered:** January 2024 – December 2025

---

## Executive Summary

Over the last two years the business has grown revenue steadily — ₹21.70 Cr in total sales and ₹3.33 Cr in profit, a 15.35% overall margin — but profit is not keeping pace with sales. Sales grew 4.27% year-over-year while profit grew only 2.19%, and the dashboard makes clear why: a small set of practices, concentrated in one category and one discount range, are quietly eating into margin even as the top line looks healthy. At the same time, one category — Technology — is doing the heavy lifting on profit and represents the clearest growth opportunity. The story below connects these threads rather than treating each chart as a separate fact.

## What Is Performing Well

Technology is the engine of this business. It generates 70.9% of sales but 84.2% of profit, at a 17.6% margin — comfortably the best of the three categories — and that strength is consistent across all four of its sub-categories (Phones, Accessories, Copiers, Machines), not driven by one outlier product line. The South region leads on both sales and profit, and Corporate customers, while not the largest segment, are the most efficiently profitable, with the highest margin (13.31%) and one of the lowest return rates (4.00%) of any segment. Same Day shipping also stands out operationally: fastest delivery (0.4 days) and the lowest return rate (2.49%) of any shipping mode, suggesting that when goods arrive quickly and as expected, customers are satisfied.

## What Is Underperforming

Furniture is the clearest underperformer. It accounts for nearly a quarter of sales but only 10.7% of profit, at a 5.8% margin — roughly a third of the company average. Two sub-categories within it, Bookcases (4.06% margin) and Tables (4.82% margin), are the weakest performers in the entire catalog, and they also carry the highest discounts (15–16%) and the highest return rates (8.4% and 6.2%). The East region also lags operationally: it has the fewest orders, the slowest average delivery (4.28 days, about a day longer than other regions), and the highest return rate (4.91%) — pointing to a fulfillment, not a demand, problem.

## Risks Visible on the Dashboard

The single biggest risk is uncontrolled discounting. The relationship between discount level and profit margin is strongly negative (correlation −0.60): margin falls from 18.98% at 0% discount to a **loss-making −4.40%** once discounts exceed 31%. Of the 288 orders that lost money outright, 87% were discounted at 21% or more, and most of those loss-making orders sit in Furniture's Bookcases and Tables. In other words, the category that most needs margin protection is also the one being discounted hardest — a self-reinforcing risk. A secondary, smaller risk is shown in delivery satisfaction: orders that take 7+ days to ship have the lowest average customer rating (3.89) of any delivery bucket, a leading indicator of dissatisfaction that may not yet have shown up fully as returns or churn.

## Opportunities Visible on the Dashboard

Technology's consistency across sub-categories (all clustered at 17–18% margin with ~3% return rates) suggests it can absorb further investment — inventory depth, marketing spend, or campaign-channel allocation — with predictable returns, rather than being a category-level bet on a single hot product. Email as a campaign channel quietly produces the highest margin of any channel (13.33%) despite being mid-sized in volume, suggesting room to grow that channel's share of acquisition spend. Corporate customers, with the best margin and a low return rate, represent a segment worth deeper account investment even though they are not currently the largest revenue segment.

## Recommended Business Actions

1. **Cap or gate discounts above 20%**, with mandatory approval beyond that threshold, since this is where margin turns structurally unsafe.
2. **Run a targeted discount-and-quality review on Bookcases and Tables** — the two sub-categories responsible for the bulk of loss-making orders and the highest Furniture returns.
3. **Investigate East region's fulfillment setup** (carrier mix, warehouse coverage) to close the one-day delivery gap and bring its return rate in line with other regions.
4. **Increase investment behind Technology**, given its consistent, repeatable, above-average margin across all sub-categories.
5. **Set a margin target alongside the revenue target** for 2026 planning, since the current growth pattern (sales outpacing profit) will continue to compress margin if left unmanaged.

## Limitations of the Dashboard

This dashboard is built on order-level transactional data and does not include cost-of-returns handling, marketing spend by channel, or inventory/holding costs — so "profit" here reflects gross order economics, not fully loaded category P&L. Customer rating and campaign channel fields have a small number of missing values (32 and 24 records respectively) that were excluded from those specific averages rather than imputed. The data covers two calendar years only, which limits long-range trend or multi-year seasonality conclusions. Return rate is based on a binary return flag and does not capture return reason, so the "why" behind Furniture's high returns is inferred, not directly observed.

## Suggested Next Analysis

A follow-up analysis should pull return-reason data (if available) for Furniture orders to confirm whether returns are quality-driven, fit/sizing-driven, or discount-driven (i.e., clearance-grade stock). A discount-elasticity study on Furniture specifically — modeling expected sales loss against margin gain at a 15% discount cap — would help leadership quantify the trade-off before implementing Recommendation 1. Finally, a deeper dive into East region's carrier-level delivery times (rather than the region-level average shown here) would help pinpoint whether the delay is one carrier, one warehouse, or a structural regional gap.
